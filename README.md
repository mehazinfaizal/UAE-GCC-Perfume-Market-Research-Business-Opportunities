## Consumer Segmentation

### By Gender - UAE

Unisex perfumes lead the UAE market, driven by the country's diverse and cosmopolitan population. Consumers value gender-neutral appeal, clean scent profiles, and everyday convenience.

| Segment | Status | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Male | Largest share | Oud-heavy, smoky, woody profiles |
| Female | Second | Floral, fruity, oriental blends |
| Unisex | Fastest growing | CAGR 7.4%; hybrid scents (floral + oud, amber + musk) |

### By Gender - GCC

| Segment | Share | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Women | 57.46% | Floral, fruity, rose, gourmand, oriental blends dominant |
| Men | — | Bestsellers: Rasasi La Yuqawam, Arabian Oud Kalemat |
| Unisex | Rapidly emerging | MFK Aqua Universalis, ByShams, niche local brands |

### By Age Group - UAE

Around 60% of the UAE population falls in the 14–40 age group with significantly higher purchasing power.

| Age Group | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| 21–40 years | Largest share (45%) | Daily usage; working expats & millennials |
| Below 20 | Fastest growing | Strong luxury fragrance demand |
| 41–60 years | Steady segment | Loyal, repeat purchasers |
| Above 60 | Smaller segment | Niche, heritage-driven preferences |

## Product Type Analysis

### GCC

| Type | Market Share | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| Eau de Parfum | 65.38% | Long-lasting; ideal for GCC climate; EdP-first strategy by most brands |
| Eau de Toilette | Growing | CAGR 4.83% (2025–2030); preferred by younger buyers for daily wear |
| Attars & CPOs | Stable | High cultural relevance; strong repeat purchase in Saudi, UAE, Oman |
| Hybrid formulations | Trending | Western accords + Arabic DNA; Gen Z & millennial focus |

### UAE - Top 3 Winning Categories

| Category | Why It Wins |
|----------|------------|
| Niche Perfumes | High uniqueness; strong youth demand; suited to Dubai's luxury retail culture |
| Attars & Oils | Strong cultural relevance; high repeat purchase rate; perceived premium value |
| Luxury Perfumes | Premium imports + local niche brands; strong mall & duty-free demand |

> Oud remains the most preferred scent - symbolising luxury, pride, and tradition. Arabic perfumes are perceived as premium, exclusive, and identity-driven, attracting both locals and tourists.

## Brand Identity Trends

**Arab Fusion DNA**
UAE brands merge traditional Arabian profiles with modern scent DNA - oud-focused, high oil concentration for 18–24 hrs longevity.

**Storytelling & Social**
New generations seek unique brand stories and strong social media engagement as key purchase drivers.

**Sustainability**
Emerging as a brand differentiator for younger, conscious consumers - particularly in UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Qatar.
